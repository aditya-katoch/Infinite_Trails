Manali Dharamshala & Dalhousie 06 Nights & 07 Days

DAY 1

Delhi – Manali (540 Km 12-13 hrs)

▼
  • On arrival at Delhi Railway Station / Airport you will be met, assisted and drive to Manali .It will take around 12- 13 hrs. To reach Manali by surface from Delhi .Manali is named after the Sanatan Hindu lawgiver Manu. The name Manali is regarded as the derivative of 'Manu-Alaya' which literally means ‘The abode of Manu'. Legend has it that sage Manu stepped off his ark in Manali to recreate human life after a great flood had deluged the world. Manali lies in the North of Kullu Valley. The valley is often referred to as the 'Valley of the Gods'. Old Manali village has an ancient temple dedicated to sage Manu.
  • Overnight Stay at hotel.
DAY 2

Solang Valley & Manali City Tour

▼
  • Morning at leisure. Post breakfast proceed for day excursion to Solang Valley- A splendid valley between Solang village and Beas Kund which offers the view of glaciers and snowcapped mountains and peaks which has fine ski slopes.
  • Post lunch drive back to town visit to Vashisht Hot Water Springs and Temple, Van Vihar, Club House, Hidimba Temple and The Mall Road of Manali. Also visit Naggar castle, Roerich art gallery.
DAY 3

Manali - Dharamshala (233 Km 6-7hrs)

▼
  • Morning drive from Manali to Dharamshala. It’s an 8–9-hour long journey drive to Mcleodganj (Dharamsala). "Dharamsala" literally means "a spiritual dwelling" and in loose translation as a shelter or rest house for pilgrims and travelers. Dharamsala came on the world map in 1959 with the arrival of the Dalai Lama and the Tibetan Government in Exile. Currently, it is a very popular hang-out for foreigners, backpackers and students of Buddhism. A large proportion of the population is Tibetan rather than Indian, and the whole town feels totally different to anywhere else in India for this reason. Behind the town to the north runs a ridge of mountains, part of the foothills. On arrival check in to hotel.
DAY 4

Dharamshala City Tour

▼
  • Post breakfast proceed for the sightseeing tour of Dharamshala. Includes visit to His Holiness the Dalai Lama's monastery Mcleodganj, St. John Church,Bhagsu Nag temple, Bhagsu Nag Waterfall, Naddi, Dal Lake, Dharamshala Cricket Stadium, War Memorial and Mall Road at Mcleod Ganj.
DAY 5

Dharamshala to Dalhousie (120 Km/4hrs.)

▼
  • Morning drive from Dharamshala to Dalhousie. It’s a 3–4-hour drive from Dharamshala. Dalhousie is located in Chamba district at an elevation of more than 6400 feet above sea level. Dalhousie is a treasure trove of ancient Hindu temples, British architecture, traditional handicrafts, art and culture. It is situated on five different hills amidst fragrant pines, rhododendrons and oak trees.
DAY 6

Excursion to Kalatop & Khajjiar

▼
  • Morning post breakfast check out from hotel and proceed for excursion to Kalatop and Khajjiar, India's Mini Switzerland, a beautiful glad with the forest at its finger.
  • Dating from the 12th century, Khajji Nag Temple is dedicated to a serpent god. Nearby, the high-altitude Khajjiar Lake is surrounded by cedar forest. West, Kalatop Wildlife Sanctuary is home to animals including deer and bears.
DAY 7

Dalhousie - Chandigarh (340 Km/8hrs.)

▼
  • After breakfast Check out from the Hotel and drive back to Chandigarh.
