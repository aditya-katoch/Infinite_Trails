Spiti Valley Tour 08 Nights-09 Days Ex- Chandigarh

About the tour

Get ready for an unforgettable adventure! Our Spiti Tour takes you through the breathtaking Himalayas, where you'll discover ancient monasteries, charming villages, and stunning landscapes. Experience the unique culture of Spiti Valley, meet friendly locals, and try delicious Tibetan cuisine. Enjoy thrilling activities like trekking, camping, and exploring scenic lakes. With its serene beauty and warm hospitality, Spiti Valley will leave you with lifelong memories. Join us for an adventure of a lifetime Witness the slow transformation from lush green hills of Kinnaur to barren brown landscapes of Spiti and then again to the greens of Manali will leave you spellbound. A drive from the highest village to the last village is totally worth remembering as you breathe in the fresh mountain air while sitting on the isolated slopes of the last village surrounded by the magnificent landscapes. Feel the chills as you look down upon a tiny truck from Asia‚Äôs highest suspended Chicham bridge or cross the manually operated cable ropeway to Kibber village, Chandartal Lake, Spiti makes a perfect powerpack for an adventurous vacation.

DAY 1

Chandigarh to Narkanda Via Shimla (175 Kms)

‚ñº
  • Begin your journey early to avoid traffic in Chandigarh. Take the Himalayan Expressway and head towards the beautiful Himalayas.
  • On the way, you will be passing through Shimla, a hill station in the lower Himalayan range and home to beautiful flower orchards.
  • After a short lunch break, you will head towards Narkanda, known as the fruit bowl of Himachal Pradesh.
  • Apple and cherry trees line the roadsides and the tall Deodhar fir trees spread their sweet aroma all around.
  • End the day after reaching the hotel in Narkanda.
DAY 2

Narkanda to Sangla Via Rampur& Karcham (150 Kms)

‚ñº
  • Wake up early in the morning to experience one of the most beautiful sunrises.
  • After a delicious breakfast, you will leave to Sangla for one of the most beautiful views of Kinnaur Valley.
  • Travel along the Sutlej river via Rampur till Karcham from where you will take a right turn towards Sangla following the Baspa river.
  • Overnight stay and dinner at hotel in Sangla.
DAY 3

Sangla to Kalpa via Chitkul (55 Kms)

‚ñº
  • After having a hearty breakfast, you will leave for Kalpa Enroute visiting Sucide Point.
  • Kamru Fort: Visit this ancient fort, located near Sangla village
  • Rakcham Valley: See the beautiful Rakcham valley with big mountains and green forests around it.
  • Kinner Kailash Viewpoint: Visit the viewpoint to witness breathtaking views of the Kinner Kailash range.
  • Chini Village: Explore this traditional village, known for its ancient temples and stunning views.
  • Visit Chitkul, the last village on the Indo-China border. Don't miss the famous "Hindustan ka Akhri Dhaba" for a delicious meal.
  • Drive back to Karcham and then head to Powari, a charming village surrounded by orchards and mountains.
  • At Powari take a left to ride to ReoKong Peo, which is the main town of Kinnaur district from where you will proceed to Kalpa.
  • Spend the night in the beautiful Kalpa village overlooking the Kinnaur Kailash mountains.
  • Overnight stay at a camp/ homestay in Kalpa, surrounded by nature's beauty.
DAY 4

Kalpa to Tabo via Khab, Nako, Sumdo & Gue (170 Kms)

‚ñº
  • After early morning breakfast, you will be leaving for Tabo passing through the beautiful Spiti Valley.
  • En route, you will be taking the Indo-Tiber Highway and pass through the Khab bridge that marks the confluence of Spiti and the Satluj River.
  • Take a short stopover at Nako Lake and have lunch, after which you will be entering the Spiti Valley right as soon as you cross Sumdo.
  • Just 3 Km after Sumdo, take a detour on the right through the gate to visit the 500-Year-Old Mummy in the Gue Village.
  • You will then proceed towards Tabo where you will be spending the night at a hotel.
DAY 5

Tabo to Kaza via Dhankar & Pin Valley (Kungri & Mud) (120 Kms)

‚ñº
  • Post breakfast check out from Tabo and drive to Dhankar Village. Dankhar was the old capital of Spiti for many years. On arrival you can take a hike to Dhankar lake as well.
  • Continue your drive to Kungri Gompa and Mud village. Kungri is Spiti's second oldest monastery, built around 1330 where as Mud is a cold desert region of Spiti region.
  • You will then proceed towards Kaza where you will be spending the night at a hotel.
DAY 6

Sightseeing in Kaza

‚ñº
  • Post early morning breakfast, go out to explore the spectacular beauty of Kaza includes visit to:
  • Key Monastery: A must visit tourist place in Spiti Valley, the Key Gompa is encircled by picturesque mountains and soothing valleys. A wonderful example of monastic architecture and decorated with beautiful paintings, murals, manuscripts, and wind instruments.
  • Kibber Village: Kibber is a picturesque little village on the higher regions of Spiti Valley in Himachal Pradesh. Also known as Kyibar, Kibber is known for the Kibber Monastery and the Kibber Wildlife Sanctuary. This small, recluse village is made of only 80 houses with approximately 366 residents.
  • Chicham Bridge: The highest bridge in Asia. The bridge is at a staggering height of 13596 ft and connects the two villages named Chicham and Kibber. Look down this one and you will find yourself gaping below, at a 1000 ft deep gorge!
  • Langza Village: includes the statue of Lord Buddha, beautiful overlook for the valley, ancient monastery and mud houses.
  • Hikkim: Hikkim village has a post office situated at an elevation of 4,400 m (14,400 ft) and this post office is the one of the highest post offices in the world.
  • Komic Village:Komic, literally meaning ‚Äúeye of a snow cock‚Äù (Ko ‚Äì snow-cock, mic ‚Äì eye), is a remote village in Spiti valley. Having its fame as the Highest village in the world connected with a motorable road, Komic is quite a destination for most people traveling to Spiti.
  • In evening return back to Kaza for overnight stay.
DAY 7

Kaza to Chandratal Lake (100 Kms) via Kunzum La

‚ñº
  • Start your day with a delicious breakfast, and then get ready to be mesmerized by the breathtaking Chandratal Lake.
  • After passing Kunzum La you will take a right before Batal to visit Chandratal Lake.
  • The beauty of this place is spectacular and Get ready for a short adventure! You'll need to leave your vehicle behind and hike for 2 kilometers to reach the lake.
  • The lake is situated in the Spiti part of the Lahaul and Spiti district and is also called the ‚ÄúMoon Lake‚Äù due to its crescent shape.
  • As night falls, the lake transforms into a celestial wonderland, with the Milky Way glittering above like a diamond-studded canvas, adding to the lake's enchanting beauty.
  • Enjoy dinner and overnight stay at the camps near Chandratal.
DAY 8

Chandratal Lake to Manali (110 Kms)

‚ñº
  • Today you will be leaving behind the Spiti Valley and drive towards Manali via Batal, Chhatru, Gramphu, Koksar & Atal Tunnel.
  • After reaching Manali you will check in to the hotel and take some rest.
  • Overnight stay at the hotel in Manali.
DAY 9

Manali to Chandigarh (300 Kms)

‚ñº
  • After breakfast, you will be transferred from Manali to Chandigarh and will be reaching Chandigarh by evening.
  • Once you reach Chandigarh, this marks the end of your memorable tour to Spiti Valley.
