DAY 2

Dharamshala – Bir Billing (65 Kms/2 Hrs)

▼
  • Morning after breakfast check out from hotel and proceed to Billing. Enroute Visit. • Baijnath templeis famous for its ancient Nagara style architecture, dedicated to Lord Shiva as Vaidyanath (the lord of physicians), and its historical significance as a Katyuri style temple built in 1204 AD. • Palampur tea gardenfamous for being the "Tea Capital of Northern India"and are known for their lush green tea plantations, which are a major tourist attraction. • Biris situated 35 km from Palampur while Billing is situated 14 km from Bir. Bir is renowned as the "Paragliding Capital of India" and is known for its natural beauty, ecotourism, spirituality, and adventure activities, particularly paragliding. One can enjoy various adventure activities in Bir Billing (Paragliding, trekking on direct payment basis.)
  • Overnight camp stay at Billing
DAY 3

Bir Billing -Dharamshala (165 Kms/7 Hrs)

▼
  •  Morning post breakfast, check out from hotel and drive towards Dharamshala. Enroute Visit to • Kangra Fortis famous for its strategic location, impressive architecture, and rich history, including being the largest fort in the Himalayas and a witness to numerous battles and invasions. Kangra Fort is one of the oldest dated forts in India&today it is a popular tourist destination. • The Masrur Temples, also referred to as Masroor Temples or Rock-cut Temples at Masrur, is an early 8th-century complex of rock-cut Hindu temples in the Kangra Valley of Beas River in Himachal Pradesh, India. The temples face northeast, towards the Dhaula Dhar range of the Himalayas. The complex is dedicated primarily to Lord Shiva. • Norbu inga Institute founded in 1995 by Kelsang and Kim Yeshi at Sidhpur, near Dharamshala, India, is dedicated to the preservation of the Tibetan culture in its literary and artistic forms. Its opening time is 9 am to 5 pm .
